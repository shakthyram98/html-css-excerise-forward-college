Kyoto

Kyoto is the cultural and historical heart of Japan, serving as the nation's imperial capital for over 1,000 years (794–1868). Located in the Kansai region on Honshu island, it is renowned as a "museum-city" that offers a glimpse into old Japan, characterized by thousands of traditional Buddhist temples, Shinto shrines, palaces, and, often, geisha in the historic Gion district.

Osaka

Osaka is Japan's third-largest city and the energetic, fun-loving capital of the Kansai region. Often called "[Kuidaore]" (to eat oneself bankrupt), it is renowned as Japan's kitchen, famous for street food like takoyaki and okonomiyaki.

Okinawa

Okinawa is Japan's southernmost prefecture, consisting of a chain of over 160 islands (the Ryukyu Islands) that stretch between Kyushu and Taiwan. Known as the "Hawaii of Japan," it features a distinct subtropical climate, white-sand beaches, and vibrant coral reefs, making it a popular destination for snorkeling and diving.
